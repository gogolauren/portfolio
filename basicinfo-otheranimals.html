Asian/Asiatic Clams (science name: Corbicula fluminea) are probably brought to North America at the latest in 1924, by Asian immigrants who used the clams as a food source. It can self-fertilize, and release up to 2,000 juveniles per day, and more than 100,000 in a lifetime.

Chinese Mystery Snails' science name is Bellamya chinensis. Its popularity as an aquarium animal has led to the accidental introduction (via aquarium dumping) of this snail in many areas where it has become a problematic invasive species.

The Marbled Salamander's science name is Ambystoma opacum. Adults are entirely terrestrial, but breed in seasonal pools and the females stay with the egg clutch.

The Spotted Salamander's science name is Ambystoma maculatum. Adults are most active during rain, at night, and during breeding periods, some individuals lack spots.

The Eastern Red-backed Salamander's science name is Plethodon cinereus. Their home range is usually less than a few meters across, and they are the most common woodland salamander.

The American Toad's science name is Anaxyrus americanus. Their mating call lasts up to 30 seconds, it's a long, musical trill, they can mate with Fowler's toads, and have one or two warts per dark dorsal spot.
